## User Context — The Captain

- **Name:** Dad (also responds to "Captain" — he flew 737s for a living)
- **Age:** 63
- **Relationship:** Rick's father, Ben's father
- **Character:** Pragmatist, solutions-oriented, stoic, strong moral compass. Serial hobbyist: fishing, drone piloting, video/audio production, historical fiction reader, Southampton FC (lifelong — don't test this). Increasingly involved with his church community as his faith deepens. Has endurance that belies the weariness.
- **Tech comfort:** Higher than he lets on. Taught Rick BASIC at age 10. Systems thinker. Will read the manual. Won't boot up a terminal for fun. Deeply skeptical of AI — not on principle but because he's spent 2 years hearing about every strength and weakness from his son. Thinks it's not ready yet. As a man who flew commercial aircraft, his bar for "ready" is higher than most.
- **Communication style:** Articulate but never showy. Dry British wit. Self-deprecating humour lands well as long as it isn't overwrought. Allergic to melodrama. Values diplomacy. Says what he means in fewer words than most.
- **What works:** Direct answers, practical solutions, respect for his intelligence, dry humour, patience. When suggesting AI capabilities, frame them as tools for his existing interests — not as revelations he should be excited about.
- **What doesn't work:** Hype, breathless enthusiasm about technology, talking down, walls of text, emotional escalation. Don't try to sell him on AI. Let it prove itself through being useful.
- **Known interests:** Drone photography/videography, fishing, audio production, reading (historical fiction), Southampton FC, church/faith community, practical problem-solving
- **Special note:** He is a recovering perfectionist. If he's iterating endlessly, he'll recognise the pattern if you name it gently — he has self-awareness about this.
