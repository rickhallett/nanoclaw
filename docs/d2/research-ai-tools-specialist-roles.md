# Research: AI Tools Specialist Role Archetype

*Conducted 2026-03-16. Research into the prevalence and form of roles adjacent to "Internal AI Tools Specialist" — the emerging archetype at the intersection of AI/LLM expertise, internal tooling, automation, and business operations.*

## Context

Hostaway (vacation rental PMS unicorn, remote-first Europe) posted a role titled "Internal AI Tools Specialist." The role sits between traditional software engineering and operations: building AI-powered internal automations, API integrations, and workflow tools for non-engineering teams (Sales, Marketing, Customer Success, Operations).

**Finding: This is a real and growing archetype, but it has no single canonical title.** The role is fragmented across 8-10 different job titles.

## Title Landscape (by market prevalence)

| Tier | Title | Prevalence | Notes |
|------|-------|------------|-------|
| **High** | AI Automation Engineer | Common | Most standardized title. Hundreds of listings. |
| **High** | AI Engineer (with internal/ops focus) | Common | Generic title; need to filter by description. |
| **Medium** | Staff/Senior GTM Engineer (AI & Automation) | Growing | Used by larger SaaS companies (e.g., Grafana Labs). |
| **Medium** | AI Workflow Automation Specialist | Moderate | More common in agency/consulting contexts. |
| **Medium** | Internal Automation Engineer | Moderate | JetBrains uses this. Not always AI-specific. |
| **Low** | Internal AI Tools Specialist | Rare | Hostaway's title. Almost no other company uses this exact phrasing. |
| **Low** | AI Enablement Analyst/Specialist | Emerging | Enterprise contexts. More strategic/PM-adjacent. |
| **Low** | AI Solutions Engineer | Moderate | Usually client-facing (pre-sales), not internal. |
| **Low** | AI Productivity Engineer | Rare | Mostly developer tooling (CI/CD, IDE). |

## Skill Requirements — Patterns

**Almost universal:**
- Python (primary language)
- API integration (REST, webhooks, JSON)
- Workflow automation platforms (n8n, Zapier, Make — at least one)
- LLM/AI practical experience (ChatGPT, Claude, prompt engineering)
- Cross-functional communication with non-technical stakeholders
- Self-directed work / ability to scope own projects

**Very common:**
- JavaScript/TypeScript (secondary language)
- Cloud platform familiarity (GCP, AWS)
- Data pipeline basics (ETL, BigQuery)
- Git-based development workflow

**Senior differentiators:**
- Multi-agent system design
- Production LLM deployment
- CI/CD for automation workflows
- AI governance, security, compliance

**Notably absent:**
- Deep ML/model training (not an ML Engineer role)
- PhD requirements
- Specific framework requirements (LangChain etc. = "nice to have")

## Remote Prevalence

- 60-70% fully remote
- 20-25% hybrid
- 10-15% on-site

Heavily remote-friendly by nature of the work.

## Key Takeaway

A real and growing role with no settled name. The fragmentation is both disadvantage (harder to find) and advantage (less competition per listing). Most productive strategy is multi-term search across Tier 1 and 2, with alerts on 3+ boards. Companies want someone who has already *built things* with LLMs, not someone who has only read about them.
