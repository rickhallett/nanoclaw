Harvard Business School field experiment, 776 professionals at Procter & Gamble. Individuals with AI were 3x more likely to produce ideas in the top 10% of quality. AI broke down functional silos: R&D people produced more commercially viable ideas, marketing people produced more technically grounded ideas. A single person with AI matched a two-person team without it. The coordination cost of combining perspectives was consumed by the tool instead of the organisation.
